Customer Churn Predictor

Using a telecom company dataset as an example

BRIEF EXPLANATIONS

The prediction is computed using the machine learning algorithm - Gradient Boosting.
For this supervised learning problem, there are total over 7000 training examples with labels. 10 fold Cross validation was performed to ensure no overfitting. Model performance is measured by Area Under the Curve (AUC). The validated model achieved average AUC score of 0.846.

ROC curve
In this problem all the input fields are treated as potential features, while the label is whether the customer churns.
Each feature is not equally important on impacting the result. Using Random Forest Classifier, features' relative importance is ranked as shown below:

Features Importances
Obviously 3 features are more important than others. The top 3 important features are: Total Charges (feature #3 on plot), Monthly Charges (feature #2 on plot) and tenure (feature #1 on plot). In addition, the correlations of those features is as shown in the heat map below:

Heat map of features
It's not surprising to see that the charges to a customer, either total wise or monthly wise, are the determining factor that impact customer's loyaty.
Interestingly, the tenure plays the next critical role. The longer a customer has been work with a company, the lower possibility this customer will quit.
In another word, newly subscribed customer have higher potential to leave. With this finding, some promotion offers may be provided especially targeting the newer customers...
